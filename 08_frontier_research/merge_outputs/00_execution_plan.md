## 2. Exact output contract for worker outputs

7. CSV-ready evidence-ledger rows (block fenced as csv, header = ledger header).

### 2.3 Candidate-DB CSV row contract (26 columns, exact header order)
These rows are produced by workers as *proposals*; only the merger/centralized roles write them into the master file.

```
candidate_id,domain,candidate_name,one_sentence_product,system_boundary_shift,extreme_metric,first_customer,buyer_title,customer_pain,current_workaround,why_current_solution_fails,hardware_stack,software_stack,prototype_2026_2028,china_wedge,us_wedge,cleanroom_dependency,capex_band,reg_export_risk,competitors,defensibility,kill_criteria,evidence_status,key_sources,worker_id,notes
```

Field rules:
- `candidate_id`: `<PREFIX>-NNN` zero-padded, unique within the worker (e.g. `PWR-001`). Prefix = worker prefix in §1.
- `worker_id`: the prefix (e.g. `PWR`).
- `evidence_status`: one of `CLEARED | CLEARED_WITH_WEAKNESS | HOLD | KILL | WATCH | WEAK_SIGNAL_ONLY`. Workers default to `WATCH`/`HOLD`/`WEAK_SIGNAL_ONLY`; only the source auditor may upgrade to `CLEARED`/`CLEARED_WITH_WEAKNESS`.
- `key_sources`: source_ids from this worker's ledger rows, `;`-separated.
- `capex_band`: qualitative band (e.g. `<$2M | $2–10M | $10–50M | >$50M`).
- `cleanroom_dependency`: `none | shared-fab-outsourcable | dedicated-cleanroom-required`.
- Commas inside a field must be quoted (`"..."`); no raw newlines inside a field. Keep every column populated (no blanks for the candidate-quality-bar fields).
- Every candidate must satisfy the **CLAUDE.md candidate quality bar**: one-sentence product, boundary shift, extreme metric, first high-end customer, buyer title, current workaround, why current solution fails, prototype path 2026–2028, China feasibility, US expansion path, defensibility, kill criteria, source evidence.

### 2.4 Mandatory per-candidate fields (mirrors quality bar; all required)
candidate_id; candidate_name; one-sentence product; system boundary shift; extreme metric; first high-end customer; buyer title; pain; current workaround; why current solution fails; hardware stack; software/control stack; prototype path 2026–2028; China wedge; US wedge; cleanroom dependency; capex band; regulatory/export risk; competitors; defensibility; kill criteria; evidence status; key sources.

### 2.5 Evidence-ledger CSV row contract (20 columns, exact header order)
```
source_id,worker_id,date_accessed,source_title,source_url_or_citation,domain,source_tier,source_type,peer_reviewed_status,impact_quality,publication_date,geography,topic,claim_supported,direct_support,confidence,limitations,count_toward_evidence,used_in_candidate_ids,notes
```
Field rules:
- `source_id`: `<PREFIX>-SNNN` (e.g. `PWR-S001`).
- `source_tier`: `1 = peer-reviewed/official primary`, `2 = authoritative non-article (gov/standards/national-lab/filing/procurement)`, `3 = journalism/timing-only`, `4 = company claim`, `5 = blocked/weak-signal`.
- `source_type`: e.g. `peer_reviewed_journal | peer_reviewed_conference_no_jif | govt_policy | standard | national_lab_report | public_filing | procurement | industry_assoc_stat | journalism | company_page | preprint_blocked`.
- `peer_reviewed_status`: `yes | no | n/a_official`.
- `impact_quality`: `high | moderate | n/a_official | low_downgraded`.
- `direct_support`: `yes | partial | no` (does the source directly support the cited claim).
- `count_toward_evidence`: `yes | no`. Preprints, company pages, market reports, blogs = `no` and must be flagged `WEAK_SIGNAL_ONLY` until replaced.
- `used_in_candidate_ids`: `;`-separated candidate_ids.

### 2.6 Company-radar contract (Worker 6) — `company_radar_database.csv` row shape (11 cols)
```
company_id,company_name,domain,boundary_shift,extreme_metric,first_market,source_urls,china_analogue_feasibility,adjacent_startup_wedge,why_direct_copy_fails,notes
```
- `company_id`: `RAD-NNN`. 20 profiles, ≥4 each in power, semi/thermal/packaging, biomed/neurotech, industrial/robotics, extreme/fusion/cryo/high-field.
- Company pages are company claims only; corroborate with journalism/filings/procurement/peer-reviewed where possible.

## 5. Source-audit gate criteria (applied later by `peer-reviewed-source-auditor`, prompt 09)

Per `source_quality_policy_peer_reviewed_high_impact.md`:
- **Academic counts only if** peer-reviewed AND (JCR Q1/Q2 | JIF pctile ≥50 | Scopus CiteScore pctile ≥50 | SJR Q1/Q2 | top peer-reviewed conference). Label conferences `peer_reviewed_conference_no_jif`.
- **Does not count:** arXiv/bioRxiv/medRxiv/SSRN/OSF/Research Square/TechRxiv/ChemRxiv, GitHub/HF/Kaggle/PapersWithCode/OpenReview (unless final venue cited), Medium/Substack/Reddit/Wikipedia/LinkedIn/social, generic market-report aggregators.
- **Non-article evidence valid without impact factor:** official policy/regulation/standards/government stats/public filings/procurement/national-lab reports/industry-association stats.
- **Company pages = company claims only.** Market reports = triangulation only (never customer-pain proof).
- Evidence-status enum (exact): `CLEARED | CLEARED_WITH_WEAKNESS | HOLD | KILL | WATCH | WEAK_SIGNAL_ONLY`.
- If a candidate's core technical OR core customer-pain claim has no direct Tier-1/2 (peer-reviewed/official) support → `HOLD` or `WEAK_SIGNAL_ONLY`. Generic / undefendable → `KILL`.
- **Hard gate:** no candidate may enter the ranked top list if its core technical or customer-pain claim is supported only by preprints, company claims, market reports, or weak journalism.

Auditor outputs: source-audit memo; cleared list; hold list; kill list; source-repair list; CSV-ready `source_issue_tracker.csv` rows; updated evidence-ledger rows.

## 6. Merge / dedupe plan (`merger-synthesizer`, prompt 08)

1. Run `scripts/merge_worker_outputs.py` → builds `merge_outputs/combined_worker_outputs.md` from all worker files.
2. Read combined file + each `worker_outputs/*.md`.
3. Deduplicate across lanes; collapse near-duplicates into one canonical candidate, preserving the strongest sources and all distinct wedges.
4. Build a **cluster map** (group candidates by engine-layer theme) and a duplicates-merged log.
5. Reject candidates that are generic/too-weak per CLAUDE.md anti-patterns; record reasons.
6. Assign final consistent candidate_ids; write CSV-ready rows for `frontier_candidate_database.csv` (§2.3 header) and `source_evidence_ledger.csv` (§2.5 header).
7. List issues requiring source audit.
8. **Do not score, do not rank top 12, no final strategy.**

CLAUDE.md anti-patterns to reject at merge: generic AI dashboard; monitoring-only (unless it changes control/treatment); TAM-only claim; "China clone of US company" without a defensible wedge; cleanroom-heavy with no outsourcing path; regulatory speculation as fact; company claim treated as independent evidence.
