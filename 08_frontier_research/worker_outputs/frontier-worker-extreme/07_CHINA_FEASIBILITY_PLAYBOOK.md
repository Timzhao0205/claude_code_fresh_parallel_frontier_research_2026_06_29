# 07 — China Feasibility Playbook

For every frontier opportunity, evaluate China feasibility with this lens.

## 1. China advantage factors

- Manufacturing supply chain depth
- Existing domestic OEM demand
- Industrial policy support
- Local capital availability
- Engineering talent and supplier density
- Ability to pilot with non-US customers
- Faster hardware iteration

## 2. China risk factors

- Commoditization and price compression
- State-owned buyer procurement complexity
- IP leakage / copycat pressure
- Export-control and dual-use exposure
- Need for domestic entity / local partner
- US expansion trust barrier
- Medical or aviation certification barriers

## 3. Feasibility labels

Use exactly one:

- China-first attractive
- China-first possible but dangerous
- China-first not recommended
- US-first then China manufacturing
- Dual-track from day one

## 4. Do not recommend direct clones

A China analogue should be:

- adjacent subsystem
- narrower beachhead
- better China-local supply chain
- lower certification burden
- more defensible than a commodity copy

Example: instead of “build Hinetics in China,” consider:

- cryogenic motor-drive test platform
- cryogenic power electronics
- HTS current leads
- high-speed integrated motor-inverter for marine/industrial
- superconducting power bus termination and quench monitoring
